## The Turtle Trail: Mon Repos and Bundaberg's Marine Magic

Bundaberg's Mon Repos Turtle Rookery is the largest loggerhead turtle nesting site in the South Pacific, attracting thousands of eco-tourists annually. In the 2025 nesting season, over 500 endangered loggerhead turtles nested here—the highest since the 1970s—creating unforgettable encounters for visitors who witness hatchlings racing to the sea.

* **Conservation and Adventure:** Over 90% of Mon Repos visitors report increased support for marine conservation after their experience, blending education with excitement.
* **Seasonal Surge:** Historical data shows up to 25,000 visitors per season in peak years, with nightly tours drawing nature enthusiasts from around the world.
* **Eco-Friendly Appeal:** In 2026, focus on sustainable tourism aligns with global trends, making Bundaberg a must-visit for responsible travelers seeking authentic wildlife interactions.

## World-Class Diving: Accessing the Great Barrier Reef from Bundaberg

As the closest mainland access point to the Southern Great Barrier Reef, Bundaberg offers some of the world's best diving conditions at sites like Lady Musgrave Island. Crystal-clear lagoons, vibrant coral gardens, and diverse marine life—including turtles, rays, and reef sharks—make it a diver's paradise.

* **Pristine Conditions:** Lady Musgrave's protected lagoon boasts visibility up to 30 meters, ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving day trips from Bundaberg Port Marina.
* **Visitor Influx:** The broader Great Barrier Reef sees around 2.13 million visitor days annually (2023 data from the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority), with international travel projected to exceed pre-pandemic levels by 2025.
* **Bundaberg's Share:** In 2025, the Southern Great Barrier Reef welcomed 88,000 international visitors—up 9.8% year-on-year—with 27,000 specifically to the Bundaberg region, many for holiday diving and snorkeling.

Globally, the Reef attracts over 2 million tourists yearly, contributing $5.89 billion to Australia's economy and employing 69,000 people. For Bundaberg, this translates to $139.4 million in international spend, driven by longer stays averaging 22.2 nights.
